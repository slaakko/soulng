Grammar Notation

A grammar consists of rules that have a name and body:

rule → body
  • Language keywords are in bold font.
  • Terminal symbols are in monospace font.
  • The names of the rules are in italic font.
  • Expression e0 | e1 represents alternatives: either e0 occurs or e1 occurs.
  • Expression e0 e1 represents a sequence: e0 occurs and then e1 occurs.
  • Expression e* represents Kleene closure: e occurs zero or more times.
  • Expression e+ represents positive closure: e occurs one or more times.
  • Expression e? represents optionality: either e occurs or not.
  • Expression [a-z] represents a character class: a single character belonging to the class occurs.
  • Grammar operator '|' has least precedence, then comes catenation. Operations '*', '+' and '?' have greatest precedence. Precedence can be overridden or clarified using parentheses.
